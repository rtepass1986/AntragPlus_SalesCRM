# Jordan Belfort Straight Line Discovery Call Script

## Script Type: Discovery/Qualification Call
**Duration**: 15-20 minutes  
**Goal**: Qualify prospect, build certainty, set up demo/next steps

---

## 1. OPENING (First 4 Seconds) âš¡

**YOU HAVE 4 SECONDS TO ESTABLISH AUTHORITY**

> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. How are you today?"

*[Wait for response, mirror their energy]*

> "Great! [Name], the reason for my call is [brief value prop]. Did I catch you at a good time for about 5-10 minutes?"

**Key Points**:
- Strong, confident tonality
- Smile while talking (they can hear it)
- Speak with certainty and authority
- Get permission to continue

---

## 2. RAPPORT BUILDING (30-60 Seconds) ðŸ¤

**DON'T SPEND TOO LONG HERE - GET TO BUSINESS**

> "Before we get started, [Name], I want to make sure we're not wasting each other's time. Can I ask you a couple quick questions to see if what we do might be a fit?"

*[Wait for agreement]*

> "[Insert casual rapport moment based on context - their location, industry, recent news about their company]"

**Key Points**:
- Build connection but stay brief
- Find common ground quickly
- Show you've done research
- Transition to discovery smoothly

---

## 3. DISCOVERY PHASE (5-7 Minutes) ðŸ”

**YOUR GOAL: IDENTIFY PAIN POINTS AND BUILD THE THREE TENS**

### Current Situation Questions:

> "Tell me, [Name], what are you currently using for [your solution category]?"

> "How long have you been using that? And how's that been working for you?"

> "What made you look into changing/improving that?"

### Pain Point Questions:

> "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about [their current situation], what would it be?"

> "How is that affecting your [business/team/results]?"

> "What would it mean to you personally if we could solve that?"

### Impact Questions:

> "Help me understand - what's this costing you? Not just in money, but in time, stress, lost opportunities?"

> "If you don't solve this, where do you see yourself in 6 months?"

### Decision-Making Questions:

> "Let me ask you - are you the person who makes the final decision on this, or is there someone else involved?"

> "What's your typical process for evaluating something like this?"

> "If this makes sense, what's the next step on your end?"

**Key Points**:
- Ask open-ended questions
- Listen more than you talk (60/40 rule)
- Identify emotional AND logical pain
- Qualify budget, authority, need, timeline

---

## 4. MINI-PRESENTATION (3-5 Minutes) ðŸ’¡

**TIE YOUR SOLUTION DIRECTLY TO THEIR PAIN POINTS**

> "Got it. So based on what you're telling me, here's how we help companies like yours..."

> "[Explain solution focused on BENEFITS not features]"

> "Specifically for your situation with [their pain point], what that means is [benefit tied to their pain]."

> "The reason this works so well is [proof/case study/social proof]."

### Build the Three Tens:

**Product Certainty**:
> "Companies like [similar company] were dealing with the same [pain point], and after implementing our solution, they saw [specific result] in [timeframe]."

**Salesperson Certainty**:
> "I've been helping companies in [their industry] solve this exact problem for [time period]. I've seen this work firsthand."

**Company Certainty**:
> "We've been doing this since [year], with over [number] clients, including [impressive client names]."

**Key Points**:
- Focus on benefits, not features
- Tie everything to their specific pain
- Use social proof and case studies
- Build certainty in all three areas

---

## 5. TRIAL CLOSE (Test Certainty Levels) âœ“

> "Based on what I've shared so far, on a scale of 1 to 10, how certain are you that [product] can solve [their pain point]?"

*[Listen to their response]*

**If 8-10**: Move to close  
**If 5-7**: Handle objections, provide more certainty  
**If 1-4**: Might not be a fit, qualify further

> "What would it take to get you to a 10?"

*[Address their concerns]*

---

## 6. HANDLING OBJECTIONS ðŸ›¡ï¸

### Common Objections & Responses:

**"I need to think about it"**
> "I totally understand, [Name]. Let me ask you - what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the product itself, the timing, or the investment?"

*[Isolate the real objection, then address it]*

**"It's too expensive"**
> "I hear you, [Name]. And honestly, if you felt the same way after we solve [their pain], I'd agree with you. But here's the thing - what's it costing you RIGHT NOW to not have this solved? [Quantify their pain cost]"

**"I need to talk to my [partner/boss/team]"**
> "Absolutely, [Name]. And I respect that. Let me ask - if they said yes, would YOU be ready to move forward? [Isolate their certainty] What concerns do you think they might have?"

**Key Principle**: Loop back to certainty. Every objection means certainty in one of the Three Tens is not at 10.

---

## 7. CLOSE (Ask for the Sale) ðŸŽ¯

**ALWAYS ASK FOR THE SALE - MULTIPLE TIMES IF NEEDED**

### Alternative Choice Close:
> "So [Name], we have two options: [Option A] or [Option B]. Which makes more sense for you?"

### Assumptive Close:
> "Perfect. So what I'll do is get you set up today, and you'll be able to [benefit] by [timeframe]. Sound good?"

### Direct Close:
> "So [Name], are you ready to get started?"

### Next Step Close:
> "Great! So the next step is [next step - demo, trial, contract]. I have [time slot 1] or [time slot 2] available. Which works better for you?"

**Key Points**:
- Always ask for the sale
- If they say no, loop back and rebuild certainty
- Close multiple times if needed
- Be persistent but professional

---

## 8. POST-CLOSE REINFORCEMENT âœ…

**AFTER THEY SAY YES**

> "Excellent choice, [Name]! You're going to love this. Now let me walk you through what happens next..."

> "[Outline next steps clearly]"

> "One thing I want you to know - I'm going to be with you every step of the way to make sure you get [the result they want]. Sound good?"

**Reduce buyer's remorse**: Reinforce their decision, outline clear next steps, build excitement

---

## TONALITY GUIDE ðŸŽµ

Remember: **It's not what you say, it's HOW you say it**

- **Confident**: Don't ask questions that sound like statements
- **Enthusiastic**: Show genuine excitement about helping them
- **Authentic**: Be yourself, don't sound like a robot
- **Certain**: You KNOW your product will help them
- **Empathetic**: You understand and care about their pain

---

## STRAIGHT LINE PRINCIPLES ðŸ“ˆ

1. **Control the Call**: You guide the conversation
2. **Gather Intelligence**: Discovery is key
3. **Build the Three Tens**: Product, Salesperson, Company certainty
4. **Present Benefits**: Features tell, benefits sell
5. **Handle Objections**: Loop back to certainty
6. **Close, Close, Close**: Always ask for the sale
7. **Take Massive Action**: Make the calls, get the reps

---

- **Pain points identified**: Minimum 2-3
- **Closing attempts**: Minimum 2-3
